Independence Day

  • Celebrated on: August 15th every year
  • Significance: Commemorates India's independence from British rule in 1947
  • Celebrations: Flag hoisting, parades, cultural programs, President's address, Prime Minister's address, and Gallantry Awards and Police Medals
  • Educational value: Teaches the younger generation about India's history, achievements, and cultural heritage
  • National pride: Fosters a sense of national pride and belonging among citizens
  • Public participation: Encourages active public participation and brings together people from different walks of life

Independence Day of India is celebrated annually on August 15th. It marks the day in 1947 when India gained independence from British rule, following the Indian Independence Act of the same year [2]. This national holiday is celebrated with flag hoisting ceremonies, cultural events, and patriotic speeches across the country.
